India sector impacts — El Niño vs La Niña
Agriculture 🌾
El Niño: Kharif sowing drops, food prices spike, farmer distress rises.
La Niña: Good harvest, surplus production, lower food inflation.
Water resources 💧
El Niño: Reservoir levels collapse. Groundwater crisis in Deccan plateau.
La Niña: Reservoirs replenished. Groundwater table recovers.
Economy 📊
El Niño: Agri GVA contracts. GDP growth trimmed 1–2%. Inflation up.
La Niña: Rural incomes rise. Agri sector supports GDP growth.

The Wildcard: Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD)
1997 paradox: The strongest El Niño of the 20th century — yet India received 102% LPA. A strongly positive IOD completely neutralized it.  |  2002 shock: Only a moderate El Niño — but negative IOD amplified it into India's worst drought in 40 years.  |  Key lesson: Watch the IMD IOD forecast in May 2026. That update will determine whether 2026 is a disaster or a near-miss.
